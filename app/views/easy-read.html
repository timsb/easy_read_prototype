Easy Read: check if you are employed or self-employed for tax

How you pay tax

How you pay tax depends on whether you are employed or self-employed.

If you are employed, your employer takes tax from your pay and sends it to HMRC for you.

If you are self-employed, you need to tell HMRC how much you earn on a Self Assessment tax return. You will get a Self Assessment bill if you owe any tax.

Employed

You will usually be classed as employed if someone else decides how and when you do your work.

Things that could mean you are employed

You work regular hours for one person or company.

The person or company you work for sets the hours and place you must work.

You get an employment contract for your work.

You get paid time off work for things like holiday, sickness and maternity leave.

The person or company you work for pays for the materials or equipment you use to do your job.

Easy read: Check if you are employed or self-employed for tax

How you pay tax

How you pay tax depends on if you are employed or self-employed.

If you are employed, the person or company you work for takes tax from your pay and sends it to the government.

If you are self-employed, you need to fill in a Self Assessment tax return to tell the government how much money you earned.

If you are self-employed and owe tax, you will get a Self Assessment bill you must pay.

Employed

You will usually be classed as employed if someone else decides how and when you do your work.

Things that could mean you are employed

You work regular hours for one person or company.

The person or company you work for sets the hours and place you must work.

You are paid for the amount of hours you work.

You get an employment contract for your work.

You get paid time off work for things like holiday, sickness and maternity leave.

The person or company you work for pays for the materials or equipment you use to do your job. This could include things like uniforms, tools, work phones or computers.

Self-employed

You will usually be classed as self-employed if you decide how and when you work.

Things that could mean you are self-employed

You can work for more than one client at the same time.

You do not have to do the work yourself. You can hire someone else to do the work for you.

You can choose the hours and place you work.

You can charge a fixed price for a job that does not depend on how long it takes do the work.

You decide how to do the work and fix any problems after you finish the job.

You pay for the materials or equipment you use to do your job. This could include things like tools, work phones or computers.

How to get help

If you need help to work out if you are employed or self-employed for tax, contact HMRC.

HMRC can explain how you should pay tax for the work you do.

f you need help to understand your rights at work, contact Acas.

Acas gives free advice on pay and work rights.
